Things to know


A report by the United Nations in 2014 states that 4 billion people have been affected by disasters since 1994, claiming 1.3 million lives and costing US$2 trillion in economic losses. A natural calamity can cause sudden hindrance to life around, leaving them helpless. It causes damage to the property, land and leads to severe pollution issues. It affects the living of people around in many direct and indirect ways by taking away their homes, work and food supplies. It can affect public health by spreading various infectious diseases. One such present case is Australian bush fires. According to the reports, more than 18 million hectares of land have burned as of mid January, taking away about 6000 buildings and 3000 homes and millions of animals. Major areas of losses are from areas: Environment- Bushfire ash is contaminating the air, water and land in australia, polluting the marine ecosystem in extreme. Also the ash is spreading on the landscapes and has even landed on school’s playgrounds, beaches and freshwater bodies. Climate- Australian bushfire is contributing to the annual carbon emissions of Australia and also the overall greenhouse gas emissions. It is a factor going to contribute in climate change and further result in such natural catastrophes. Ecology- Loss of habitat and food sources will result in further loss of animals and insects. This shows that how biodiversity is going to be affected on a large scale and in turn worsening the condition of our global food cycle. Health- Wildfires produce fine particle air pollutants causing intense air pollution. Even short term exposure to this air is going to cause many respiratory and cardiac health diseases. Along with physical health it is affecting people psychologically, as many lost their homes and livelihoods. Economic- The damage to infrastructure is going to cost a lot to the australian economy. Many of the industries like farming and tourism are facing major trouble. Wildlife- Australian forests are home to a million of species. This disaster has caused most severe damage to the wildlife. Millions of animals have died. There are many species with little population that might have been completely wiped off. Many more are at risk as they have lost their habitat and food resources.


In some cases people are not aware of the ways they can contribute or about the available opportunities. Other times, organisations are unable to hire volunteers in emergency. They might need some time to find people nearby who can reach out. These situations can create a time lag and affected people and areas cant get the aid on right time. This can worsen the damage and can result in many more after effects. Natural calamities can not be predicted neither can the need for volunteers. As no one can not anticipate when and where there is going to be a need of volunteers there can be a shortage of volunteers. Manpower plays a very important role in such circumstances beyond one’s control. In case of Australian bushfire, firefighters are facing a hard time. Such large scale impact on Australia has resulted in shortage of fire fighting force. This really means they need urgent help to doze off the fires as the rising temperatures may further escalate the situation. Also there is a need to rescue the helpless animals who are stuck in the burning forests. This shows that a crisis of aid in such situation can completely change the scenario.


In emergency situations like natural disasters, it is our responsibility as humans to help the ones affected. Being blessed with a human life we are capable of serving the society in many ways. But in case of such large scale issues such as natural disasters, there is a lack of connectivity between the ones who need help and the ones who are willing to help. If there is no proper communication there can be a shortage of resources which are actually available to rescue the needy ones. In many past cases it has been witnessed that people suffered a lot more just because they could not get the required aid on time. There are many situations when there is a sudden need of manpower but the number requirement is not met.
